Methane

Source: Me, Lance!

Source: Gas Show Room / Rinnai Infinity 32 Instant Natural Gas Hot Water Heater (50 degs)

  • Colourless, odorless gas

  • Has various practical applications

  • Is abundant. Can be found:
    • Naturally underground (the main component of natural gas)
    • Vegetable matter under water decomposing
    • Volcanoes
    • Underwater vents
    • Animals' digestive processes (termites, cows)

  • A greenhouse gas

Chemical + Atomic Properties

  • Consists of 1 carbon atom with 3 hydrogen atoms

  • Has a tetrahedral shape

  • Lighter than air

  • Easily combusts

  • Boiling point: −162°C

  • Melting point: −182.5°C

  • Considered a non-polar covalent bond (ΔEN value is aprox. 0.35)
    • ΔEN (difference in electronegativity between elements) determines the kind of bond a compound has.
    • If ΔEN < 0.5, compound is considered to be a nonpolar covalent.
    • If ΔEN ≥ 0.5, compound is considered to be a polar covalent.

Chemical Equation for Methane

Source: Elmhurst

CH4 + 2O2 → CO2 + 2H2O

1 Methane Molecule + 2 Oxygen Molecules → 1 Carbon Dioxide Molecule + 2 Water Molecules

  • Methane: 8 electrons - 4 from carbon, other 4 from each hydrogen element.

  • Oxygen: 24 electrons - 6 from each oxygen element.

  • When methane combusts, carbon dioxide and water form.

  • Like in other chemical equations, the bonds between each reactant break in order to form the byproducts.
